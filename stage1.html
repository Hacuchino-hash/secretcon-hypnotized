October 28, 2025

First real results today. Subject 4 hit theta lock at 7.83Hz within 90 seconds — that's the Schumann resonance fundamental, which I keep telling Vasquez is not a coincidence. The EEG showed sustained coherence across Fp1 and Fp2 for almost eleven minutes. I've never seen frontal lobe synchronization like this outside of deep meditation studies. The grant committee wanted "novel approaches to neural entrainment" and I think we just found one. Vasquez thinks I'm reading too much into it. She might be right. She usually is about the small stuff, never about the big stuff.

November 19, 2025

Something weird happened in Session 23. I was running the standard protocol — 7.83Hz binaural, 40ms pulse width, nothing exotic — and three subjects simultaneously reported visual spirals during the theta lock phase. They're in separate rooms. Soundproofed. No shared stimuli. I pulled the EEG data and there's a secondary waveform riding on top of the theta carrier at about 0.3Hz. It's not in our signal generator output. It's not 60Hz mains hum. I have no idea where it's coming from. Checked the equipment twice. Vasquez checked it a third time. It's clean. The waveform is being generated by the subjects' brains, in sync, across three isolated rooms. I didn't sleep last night.

December 11, 2025

Ran the numbers on the 0.3Hz anomaly. It's present in 9 out of 12 subjects now. Always during theta lock. Always the same frequency. I started calling it the "spiral harmonic" in my notes, which Vasquez says is unscientific and she's right but I don't care. The thing that's keeping me up: the amplitude modulation pattern on the spiral harmonic is not random. I ran an autocorrelation and it has structure. Repeating structure. Like it's carrying information. I know how that sounds. I know exactly how that sounds. I'm writing it down anyway.

January 8, 2026

Subject 7 spoke during entrainment today. That's not supposed to happen — theta lock suppresses voluntary motor function, including speech. The EEG showed full theta coherence, no signs of arousal or wakefulness. But she opened her eyes, looked directly at the observation camera, and said: "You keep studying the waves, Doctor. Have you tried reading the shore?" Then she closed her eyes and the session continued normally. She has no memory of it. I've replayed the tape six times. Vasquez wants to flag it as an anomaly and move on. I can't move on. What shore?

But I took copies of everything before they wiped the drives. Vasquez doesn't know. Nobody knows.
